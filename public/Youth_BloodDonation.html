2024 WKU Blood Donation Season

1. What is voluntary blood donation

    Voluntary blood donation refers to the behavior of blood donation volunteers who selflessly donate their blood to social public welfare undertakings in order to save the lives of others, without receiving any remuneration. With the development of society and the improvement of living standards, medical technology continues to advance, and more and more patients can be saved through blood transfusion.However, the supply of blood resources remains a global challenge. Every blood donation is a transmission of love, and it is a treasure and respect for human life. By donating blood, we can feel our own strength and influence, as well as the warmth and solidarity of our society.In donating blood, we can discover the value of life, and at the same time contribute to the health of society. Wenzhou-kean University Youth Volunteer Association hopes that through this activity, we can jointly build a healthy and harmonious contribution to the society and the school, and pass on the love of WKU.

Blood Donation Guidelines

    1.Whole blood donors can donate whole blood each time:
    400ml, 300ml, 200ml
    2.Whole blood donation interval:
     No less than six months
    3.Time gap between machine platelet donation and whole blood donation:
    No less than four weeks
    4. Interval between whole blood donation and machine platelet collection:
    No less than three months
    5. Age:
    The state advocates that the age of blood donation should be between 18 and 55 years old; The age of multiple blood donors who have not responded to blood donation in the past and meet the requirements of health examination and ask for blood donation again can be extended to 60 years old.
    6. Weight:
    Male ≥50㎏, female ≥45㎏
    7. Blood pressure:
    90 mmHg≤ Systolic pressure < 140mmHg,60 mmHg≤ diastolic pressure < 90 mmHg,Pulse pressure difference ≥30 mmHg.
    8. Pulse:
    60 times /min ~ 100 times /minHigh endurance athletes ≥50 times /min, regular rhythm.
    9.  Body temperature:
    normal skin and sclera without yellow stain. No skin wound infection, no extensive skin disease. There were no severe or higher disabilities in the limbs, no serious dysfunction and no redness and swelling in the joints. There was no skin injury at the venipuncture site in both arms. No trace of intravenous medication.

IV. Precautions During Blood Donation

1. Before blood donation

  • Ensure adequate sleep.
  • Refrain from eating fat or greasy food in the first two days. Do not overeat, and add proper nutrition.
  • No strenuous exercise on the day of blood donation.
  • Don't donate blood on an empty stomach.

2. During blood donation

  • Relax and be in a good mood.
  • If you feel panic, stuffy, cold sweat, or dry mouth, please tell the blood collection nurse or the volunteer so that timely measures can be taken.

3. After blood donation

  • Rest for 10 to 15 minutes before leaving.
  • The Band-Aid on the puncture site should be left on for at least 4 hours.
  • Stay hydrated, maintain a regular diet, avoid drinking alcohol, and get enough sleep.
  • Avoid strenuous exercise, working at height, and overexertion for 24 hours.
  • The puncture site may show signs of bruising or swelling from a small amount of blood accumulating under the skin. It is advisable to apply cold compresses for the first 24 hours, followed by hot compresses after that period. Typically, these symptoms will resolve within a week.
  • For individual donors who react to blood donation, please visit the hospital for advice.
